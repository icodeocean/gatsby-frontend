Hour of Code

As the use of powerful computers progresses throughout our world it has become essential to understand how computers work, and how to program them. And while there are many different tutorials on the internet that provide the lucrative idea of gaining knowledge for just 5.99 per lesson, we all know they never really help. Schools can also be a great place to learn programming, but sadly many underpriveliged districts cannot afford extra education. As experienced programmers, we understand how hard it is to find a source of education that is useful, but engaging at the same time. By combining the engaging experience of hands on learning with relatable, but experienced high schoolers, our Hour of Code provides an environment where students can find their new passion and engage in the wonderful world of programming.

Competitions

Have you ever thought about creating a coding competition between schools? You may not have, but if you now are thinking this, we welcome you to continue reading. we are attempting to build a league of schools to host competitions testing merit in programming. It will be known as the Fairfield Programming Association (FPA). To create this organization, we only need a few things: support from your institution, two student delegates from your institution, and your help maintaining the cohesiveness of the FPA during its adolescent years. These first two years are likely going to be the most challenging for this new organization, and the FPA will be tested at its very foundation. That is why we need your help, and we have outlined what you can do below.

Firstly, your institution needs to support the FPA. This is not in any financial sense, but rather in a mutually beneficial partnership. The FPA’s competitions would allow your students to create connections with students in other schools and it would give them the opportunity to thrive in a competitive environment- all while learning. All your institution would need to support the FPA is the commitment to hosting at least one competition each year. The logistics of these competitions are outlined in the below document.

You will need to elect two student delegates from your institution to help host the competitions. These delegates will be responsible for meeting in a committee with the other FPA delegates before each competition to design them so they are fair, challenging, and most importantly, fun. The logistics of the delegates and committee meetings will be outline in the below document.

Finally, your job will be to listen for any communications that the FPA sends out, and corral your students into teams for each competition. It is expected that every institution attends at least two other competitions, but that information, again, is outlined in the below document.
